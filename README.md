# Bayesian Inference for A/B Testing: An Interpretable Alternative to Frequentist Hypothesis Testing

## Overview

This repository contains the research paper **"Bayesian Inference for A/B Testing: An Interpretable Alternative to Frequentist Hypothesis Testing."**

The project explores how Bayesian statistical methods can provide a more interpretable and flexible framework for analyzing A/B experiments compared to traditional frequentist hypothesis testing.

A/B testing is widely used in product development, digital platforms, and experimentation-driven organizations. However, classical hypothesis testing methods often lead to misunderstandings of p-values, rigid decision rules, and limited interpretability. This paper examines how Bayesian inference offers a probabilistic interpretation of experimental results and allows for more intuitive decision-making.

This repository serves as an open academic resource and part of an independent research portfolio.

---

## Abstract

A/B testing is a standard methodology for evaluating changes in digital products, marketing strategies, and online platforms. Traditionally, these experiments are analyzed using frequentist hypothesis testing, which relies heavily on p-values and fixed significance thresholds. However, these approaches are often difficult to interpret and can lead to common statistical misunderstandings.

This paper explores Bayesian inference as an alternative framework for analyzing A/B tests. By modeling uncertainty using probability distributions and updating beliefs through Bayes’ theorem, Bayesian methods provide a direct probabilistic interpretation of experimental outcomes. The paper compares Bayesian and frequentist approaches conceptually and discusses the advantages of Bayesian methods for interpretability, flexibility, and decision-making in experimental analysis.

The results suggest that Bayesian inference offers a more transparent and intuitive framework for evaluating experimental results and can improve decision-making in real-world experimentation contexts.
